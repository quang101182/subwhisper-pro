Platform-by-Platform: Adding Automatic Captions

Instagram Reels

1

Create or upload your Reel

Record directly in the Instagram app or upload a pre-made video from your camera roll.

2

Tap the sticker icon and select "Captions"

In the editing screen, tap the sticker icon (top of screen), then scroll to find the "Captions" sticker. Instagram will auto-transcribe your audio.

3

Review and edit the text

Tap on individual words to correct any mistakes. Auto-transcription is around 85-90% accurate for clear English — always proofread before posting.

4

Choose a style and position

Instagram offers a few preset caption styles. You can change the font and color, and drag to reposition. Options are limited compared to dedicated tools.

TikTok

1

Record or upload your TikTok video

Use the TikTok camera or import from your gallery.

2

Tap "Captions" on the right sidebar

In the editor, tap the "Captions" button. TikTok will process your audio and generate text automatically.

3

Edit for accuracy

TikTok's auto-captions can struggle with slang, brand names, and accents. Tap each caption block to make corrections.

4

Adjust timing and position

Drag caption blocks in the timeline to fix timing issues. Reposition on screen to avoid covering your face or key visuals.

YouTube Shorts

1

Upload your Short to YouTube Studio

YouTube automatically generates captions after upload — but these appear as closed captions, not burned into the video.

2

Edit auto-generated subtitles

Go to YouTube Studio > Subtitles > Edit the auto-generated track. Fix errors and adjust timing.

3

For burned-in captions, use a third-party tool

YouTube doesn't offer burned-in caption styling for Shorts. Use a subtitle generator to hardcode captions before uploading.

Cons

Caption Styling Tips That Get Engagement

How your captions look matters almost as much as what they say. Here are battle-tested styling tips from creators who've mastered the format.

1. Use Bold, Contrasting Colors

White text with a dark outline/background works universally. Avoid light colors on light backgrounds — if a viewer has to squint, they'll swipe away. For brand consistency, use your primary brand color for keywords and white for the rest.

2. Position in the Safe Zone

Keep captions in the center-bottom third of the screen, but above the platform UI elements (like/comment buttons on TikTok, caption text on Reels). The safe zone is roughly 60-80% from the top on most platforms.

3. Use Word-by-Word Animation

The "karaoke style" where words highlight one at a time as they're spoken dramatically increases attention. Studies show animated captions get 15-20% more completion rates compared to static text blocks.

4. Keep Lines Short

Maximum 2 lines on screen, 5-7 words per line. Mobile screens are small — walls of text are overwhelming. Break sentences at natural pause points for better readability.

5. Match the Energy

Use larger, bolder text for emphasis and key points. Scale down for casual or transitional phrases. This visual hierarchy mimics natural speech patterns and keeps viewers engaged with the rhythm of your content.

Best Practices for Auto Captions on Social Media

Before we wrap up, here are some universal best practices to make the most of auto captions on social media, regardless of which tool you use.

  • Always proofread. No auto-caption tool is 100% accurate. A single embarrassing typo can undermine your credibility. Budget 2-3 minutes per video for a quick review.
  • Record clear audio. The better your source audio, the better your captions. Use a lapel mic or record in a quiet space. If that's not possible, AI tools like SubWhisper Pro handle noise far better than built-in tools.
  • Test on mobile first. Your audience watches on phones. Preview your captioned video on a phone screen to ensure text size and positioning work at small scales.
  • Be consistent. Develop a caption style (font, color, position) and stick with it. Consistency builds brand recognition and a professional look.
  • Add captions to Stories too. Instagram Stories and TikTok Stories also benefit from captions. The same engagement principles apply — people scroll through Stories on mute too.
